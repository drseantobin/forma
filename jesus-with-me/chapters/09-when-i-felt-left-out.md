## Chapter Nine
# When I Felt Left Out

> 🎨 **Chapter picture:** Ava on the outside of a big window, looking in at a cozy, glowing room full of friends — but Jesus stands beside her on her side of the glass, with his hand gently on her shoulder.

This is a hard one to explain, because nobody did anything MEAN to me. That's what made it so confusing.

There was this birthday party. A big one. With a bounce house.

And basically every girl in my class got invited. They talked about it for a WHOLE WEEK. What they were going to wear. What games there'd be. Who was sitting next to who.

I was not invited.

I don't even think they left me out on purpose. I just wasn't on the list. But knowing it wasn't on purpose did NOT make my heart feel any better.

There's a special kind of lonely where you're surrounded by people and you STILL feel like you're standing on the outside of a window, looking in at a warm room you're not in. That's how I felt at recess, standing there, pretending to be super interested in a rock on the ground.

When I got home I didn't even want to talk. I just flopped on my bed (you know the move by now).

But then I remembered the thing I learned all the way back when I was scared of the dark: *When it feels like you're alone — ESPECIALLY then — you're not.*

So I whispered it. "Jesus, I feel left out. Will you stay with me?"

And here's the honest truth, because this is an honest book: the party did not magically send me a new invitation. The lonely feeling didn't vanish like a magic trick.

But something BETTER happened. I remembered who I am to Jesus.

I am not on the outside of ANYTHING with him. There is no circle he stands in that I'm not invited into. There is no party in his heart that I'm left out of. With Jesus, I am always on the list. I am always picked. I am always wanted.

And once I remembered that, the recess circle didn't have so much power over me anymore.

Because here's the thing about being left out: it makes you feel small and forgettable, like you don't matter much.

But I've got somebody who thinks I matter SO much that he stays with me every single second of every single day and never once forgets I'm here.

And when you know you're THAT loved, one bounce house seems like a pretty small thing to miss.

(Although. I will admit. Bounce houses look really, really fun. I'm only human.)

> 💛 **A little prayer:** *Jesus, when I feel left out and forgotten, remind me that I'm always wanted by you. Thank you that with you, I'm never standing outside the window. I'm always on the list. Amen.*

> 📖 **Jesus says:** *"I've called you by name. You are mine."* (from Isaiah 43:1)
