# Chapter Nine: How I Talk to Him

By now you might be wondering something. You might be thinking: *Ava, you keep saying you talk to Jesus. But how? You cannot see him. He does not talk back out loud. So how do you actually do it?*

That is a good question. I wondered it too for a long time. So let me tell you how I figured it out, because it is easier than you think.

Talking to Jesus is called praying. But do not let the fancy word scare you. Praying is just talking. You do not have to use special words. You do not have to close your eyes, though I usually do because it helps me think. You do not have to kneel down or fold your hands a certain way, though you can if you want.

You just... talk to him. Like he is right there. Because he is.

I do most of my talking at night, in bed, in the dark. The same dark that used to scare me when I was four. Funny how that works. The thing I was once most afraid of turned into my favorite quiet time with him.

Here is what I do. I tell him about my day. The whole thing. The good parts and the embarrassing parts and the parts I would not tell anybody else. I tell him who I am worried about. I tell him thank you for stuff. Sometimes I just lie there and do not say anything at all, and that is praying too, the same way you can sit with your best friend and not talk and it is still nice.

"But Ava," you might say, "does he ever answer?"

Yes. But not usually in a big booming voice from the sky. That has never happened to me. He answers in quieter ways. Sometimes I am worried about something, and after I talk to him about it, the worry gets smaller, like the balloon letting out its air. That is an answer. Sometimes I am trying to decide if I should do something kind that feels scary, and a brave feeling shows up that was not there before. That is an answer. Sometimes the answer is just a deep-down knowing that I am not alone, and that everything is going to be okay.

You learn to notice him, the way you learn to notice anything. At first you do not see it. Then once you know what to look for, you see it everywhere.

So that is the secret, if it even is a secret.

You do not need anything special to talk to Jesus.

You just need to start. Tonight, in your own bed, in your own dark, you can say the same thing I said when I was four years old.

"Jesus, will you stay with me?"

And he will. He already is.
