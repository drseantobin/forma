## Chapter Four
# Praying When I Was Scared

> 🎨 **Chapter picture:** Ava's dark bedroom. Her coat on the hook looks like a tall, creepy monster — but Ava peeks out from her blanket, and a soft warm glow is starting to fill the room.

Let me tell you about the scariest thing in my whole house.

It is not the basement. It is not the spider that lives behind the toilet (although, honestly, that spider is huge and we have an understanding: he stays back there, I don't scream).

The scariest thing in my house is my bedroom at night, with the light off.

Here is the thing about the dark. Everything NORMAL turns into something TERRIBLE. In the daytime my coat is just a coat. At night it becomes a tall skinny guy standing very still in the corner, waiting. My backpack becomes a crouching creature. The little creak the house makes? Definitely footsteps. Definitely.

I used to do the thing where you pull the blanket all the way over your head, because everybody knows monsters cannot get you through a blanket. That is just science.

But you cannot breathe very well under a blanket, and also you get sweaty, so it is not a great long-term plan.

One night when I was really little, my dad told me a secret about the dark that I have used ever since. He said: "Ava, the dark is scary to YOU, but it is nothing to Jesus. He can see in the dark like it's the middle of the day. The dark doesn't hide anything from him. So when you're scared, you don't have to be brave all by yourself. You can just ask him to stay."

So I tried it.

I did not pull the blanket over my head. (Well, okay, I kept it at chin level. Halfway. Compromise.) And I whispered, "Jesus, you can see in the dark. Will you stay with me?"

And I want to be honest, because this is an honest book.

The dark did not turn into daytime. The light did not magically click on. The room was still dark.

But the SCARY part of the dark kind of melted. Because the dark only feels scary when you think you are alone in it. And I wasn't. The same person who can see in the dark was sitting right there with me, totally not scared, like the monster-coat was the silliest thing he had ever seen.

I am almost nine now. I am not really scared of the dark anymore. But I still say it some nights, just because I like knowing he's there.

"Jesus, will you stay with me?"

He always does.

> 💛 **A little prayer:** *Jesus, the dark can't hide anything from you. When I'm scared, remind me I'm not alone in it. Stay with me until I fall asleep. Amen.*

> 📖 **Jesus says:** *"Even the darkness is not dark to you; the night shines like the day."* (from Psalm 139:12)
