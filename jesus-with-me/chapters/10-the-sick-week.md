## Chapter Ten
# The Sick Week

> 🎨 **Chapter picture:** Ava as a small lump under a heavy blanket, a barely-touched bowl of soup beside her — and a gentle, glowing figure sitting calmly on the edge of her bed, keeping watch.

There are two kinds of sick.

There's the FUN kind, where you have a little cough, so you get to stay home and watch movies and drink ginger ale and everyone feels bad for you. That kind of sick is honestly kind of a vacation.

And then there's the OTHER kind.

The kind where your whole body aches and the light hurts your eyes and you're so tired you can't even finish your soup. The kind where you lose track of which day it is. That kind of sick is not a vacation. That kind of sick is the pits.

I had the bad kind one winter.

Fun fact: my dad actually wrote a book about being sick. It's called *The Sick Seven*. So when I got sick, he joked that I'd become the eighth one. I did not laugh, because laughing hurt. But I did smile a tiny bit, which is all I had the energy for.

Here's the thing nobody warns you about being really sick: it's LONELY. Everyone's being nice to you, bringing you water, feeling your forehead — but YOU are still the only one stuck inside the achy body. Nobody can be sick FOR you. You're kind of alone in there.

One night, when I felt the very worst, my mom sat next to my bed and held my hand. I didn't have the energy to even talk. But she said something quiet that I keep with me.

She said: "You know, when Jesus was here, he spent SO much of his time with sick people. Everybody else stayed away from them — they were scared of catching something. But not Jesus. He'd walk right up close. He'd put his hand on the people nobody else would even touch. He LIKED being near the ones who felt the worst."

I thought about that, lying there feeling like the worst.

"So he's not far away right now?" I whispered. "Even though I'm all gross and sick?"

"ESPECIALLY right now," my mom said. "This is exactly the kind of bedside he loves to sit at."

So I closed my eyes and imagined him there. On the other side of the bed from my mom. Not scared of my fever. Not waiting for me to get better and cuter and less gross. Just sitting close, the way he always has.

I got better that week. I always do. The soup started tasting good again, the light stopped stabbing my eyes, and one morning I woke up and I was just ME again.

But I didn't forget the Sick Week. Because that's the week I learned that Jesus isn't only with me on my good days, when I'm healthy and grinning at the baseball field in my gold shoes.

He's with me on my worst day. My grossest, achiest, can't-finish-the-soup day.

He sits at that bedside too.

> 💛 **A little prayer:** *Jesus, thank you that you come CLOSE to people when they feel their worst. Thank you for sitting at my bedside on the sick days and the hard days. Amen.*

> 📖 **Jesus says:** *"It's not the healthy who need a doctor, but the sick."* (from Mark 2:17)
