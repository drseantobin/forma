## Chapter Eleven
# Praying for Somebody Else (a Plot Twist)

> 🎨 **Chapter picture:** A cutaway of the house so you can see two rooms at once: Ava praying at her bedside in one room, and a soft glowing line traveling across the house to her grandma resting in another room.

So far in this book, I'll be honest, most of my prayers have been about... me.

Help ME not be scared. Help ME beat my brother. Give ME a dog named Waffles. Help ME feel better. Me, me, me.

And that's okay! Jesus WANTS me to bring him my stuff. But one day something happened that taught me prayer has a whole other setting I didn't even know about.

It was when my grandma got sick. (Same grandma from the Waffles chapter.) Real sick. The kind where the grown-ups talk in quiet voices in the kitchen and stop when you walk in.

I felt SO helpless. I was just a kid. I couldn't fix her. I couldn't make medicine. I couldn't do ANYTHING.

And then I realized — wait. There IS one thing I can do. The same thing I'd been doing for myself this whole time.

I can pray. But this time, not for me. For HER.

So every night I prayed for my grandma. "Jesus, please be with Grandma. Help her not be scared. Sit at her bedside like you sat at mine in the Sick Week. And help the doctors. And help my mom, because she's scared too, even though she pretends she's not."

And something surprised me. Praying for somebody ELSE felt different than praying for myself. When I prayed for me, it made me feel better. But when I prayed for Grandma, it made me feel... bigger. Like my small kid-sized self had actually done something real. Like I'd reached out across the whole house and put my hand on her, even from my own bed.

My grandma got a little better, and then she got to go home. I don't know exactly how much my prayers did. That's not really for me to know. But I know they did SOMETHING, because they changed ME. They turned me from a helpless little kid into somebody who could actually help.

That's when I learned the secret about praying for other people: you can't always fix somebody with your own two hands. But you can ALWAYS carry them to Jesus. And carrying somebody to Jesus is one of the most powerful things a regular kid can do.

So now my prayers aren't only about me anymore.

Well. Mostly. I still pray for the dog sometimes. Operation Waffles never fully ends.

> 💛 **A little prayer:** *Jesus, help me remember to pray for OTHER people, not just myself. When someone I love is hurting and I can't fix it, let me carry them to you. That's something even a kid can do. Amen.*

> 📖 **Jesus says:** *"Pray for each other."* (from James 5:16)
