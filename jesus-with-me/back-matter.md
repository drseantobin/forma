# A Note From Ava

Hi. It's me, Ava. The author.

If you read all the way to here, thank you. I hope you laughed a little, and I hope you maybe felt a little less alone.

I wrote this book because I wanted other kids to know what I know. That you are not alone. Not ever. Not in the dark, not on the gray days, not when you lose at chess, not when you mess up, not when you're stuck outside the bounce-house window, not even when you're sick in bed feeling like the worst.

Jesus has been with me my whole life. And he wants to be with you the same way.

You don't have to be big enough, or good enough, or smart enough. I'm only just nine, and I'm definitely not all of those things yet. He doesn't wait for you to catch up. He's just there, already, like the sky.

So here's what I want to tell you. The same thing I've said since I was four years old in the dark:

You can talk to him. Tonight. In your own bed.

You can say, *"Jesus, will you stay with me?"*

And he will.

He already is.

Love,
Ava

# Your Turn

Every chapter in my book was a time Jesus was with me.

Now it's YOUR turn to start your own book.

On the next pages, draw a time when YOU felt like you weren't alone. It can be anything — a scary night, a sad day, a really good day, a time you said sorry, a time somebody you love was sick.

That's your first chapter.
